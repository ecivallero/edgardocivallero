Introduction

This section gathers my written work: an open, diverse, and evolving archive where memory, classification, critique, and narrative intersect. All content is freely available through different platforms, each with specific goals and audiences:

  • Acta Académica, for circulation in Latin American academic circles.
  • ResearchGate, for ongoing updates and professional networking.
  • Internet Archive, as a space for open and distributed preservation.
  • Google Scholar, to track visibility and impact in academic environments.
  • Google Drive, as a full, personal, and regularly updated repository.

Among the materials available, you'll find:

  • A series of digital books on Indigenous libraries and community knowledge management.
  • Photographic works focused on memory, territory, and biodiversity.
  • Fiction works weaving together history, identity, and epistemic critique.

Materials related to sound memory, musical instruments, and endangered acoustic landscapes are part of the Instrumentarium project, and are available as books and articles, in both Spanish and English.

Between 2007 and 2022, I published most of my books through Wayrachaki Editora, a personal and independent imprint. Since 2023, this work continues —and expands— under El Zorro de Abajo Editora.

All materials are protected by copyright and distributed under Creative Commons licenses. For more information, see the copyright and disclaimer section.
