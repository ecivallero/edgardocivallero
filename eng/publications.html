Introduction

This section gathers my written work: an open, diverse, and evolving archive where memory, classification, critique, art, and narrative intersect. Rather than concentrating publications in a single platform, my work is distributed across complementary spaces, each fulfilling a specific function within the broader ecology of knowledge production and preservation.

  • Google Drive functions as working memory: drafts, editable files, and materials in flux — the living workshop where thinking happens.
  • Internet Archive operates as public memory: open access, long-term preservation, redundancy, and cultural circulation.
  • Acta Académica provides disciplinary visibility, especially within Latin American academic ecosystems, supporting citation and regional scholarly exchange.
  • Zenodo serves as the canonical reference layer, freezing specific versions, assigning DOIs, and establishing stable records for citation.

My publications are also indexed on platforms such as Google Scholar and ResearchGate, which function as secondary discovery tools rather than authoritative or curated records.

Among the materials available, you'll find:

  • A series of digital books on Indigenous libraries.
  • Photographic works focused on memory, territory, and biodiversity.
  • Fiction works weaving together history, identity, and epistemic critique.

Materials related to sound memory, musical instruments, and endangered acoustic landscapes are part of the Instrumentarium project, and are available as books and articles, in both Spanish and English.

Between 2007 and 2022, I published most of my books through Wayrachaki Editora, a personal and independent imprint focused on libraries, archives, and memory in conflict or marginal settings. Since 2023, that editorial work has expanded into two distinct but complementary lines: Wayrachaki continues to house texts on critical librarianship and information practices, while El Zorro de Abajo Editora focuses on music, orality, sound memory, and traditional instruments — especially in Latin America..

Narrative and visual works that do not follow those editorial lines (like the fiction and photographic works above) are released under Estudio paramuno, my creative studio. They are not part of any imprint, but are part of the same broader body of work.

All materials are protected by copyright and distributed under Creative Commons licenses. For more information, see the copyright and disclaimer section.
