Activities

Throughout my career, I have offered talks, workshops, seminars, collaborations, experimental sessions, and voluntary mentorship. This section brings together those actions — gestures of activation, connection, and experimentation. None are governed by contractual frameworks or commercial logic. They arise from a desire to share, to sow questions, and to explore other ways of constructing memory, knowledge, and meaning.

All the consulting work that I offered through this site over the years was consolidated in 2025 into Tela de Araña, the studio through which I now develop and provide my professional services: system diagnostics, structural and semantic design, metadata and ontology development, workflow planning, editorial and digital production, and long-term continuity strategies for organizations that require coherent and sustainable knowledge and memory systems.

 

Conversations and workshops

Talks, conversations, lectures, masterclasses, workshops, and seminars for universities, libraries, citizen projects, community groups, NGOs, professional networks, and academic institutions. Each activity fosters critical reflection on systems of knowledge management, classification, documentation, and preservation.

 

Grassroots development projects

In grassroots, trench, or marginal contexts, I support autonomous processes in popular libraries, community archives, documentation centers, independent labs, and Indigenous or activist collectives. The goal is to strengthen local knowledge, memory, and circulation practices. I do not offer packaged solutions — I work through listening, respect, and situated design.

 

Open collaborations

I participate in collaborative action-research projects and experimental initiatives grounded in equality, respect, and horizontality. Duration and format are flexible; what matters is the shared force of the action.

 

Documentation of oral histories and soundscapes

I collaborate with communities and researchers in recording, classifying, and activating unwritten memories: endangered languages, traditional music, oral testimony, archives of silence, and soundscapes. These efforts are built on trust, reciprocity, and ethical respect.

 

Participation in unconventional projects

I take part in informal networks and experimental spaces that challenge normative frameworks: unauthorized libraries, edge services, living collections, insurgent epistemologies, decolonial spaces, and semantic technologies for broken systems. These collaborations may take the form of pilot projects, experimental advisories, collective publications, or open platforms.

 

Dissemination and Activation of Knowledge

I contribute to interviews, podcasts, informal gatherings, alternative publications, and decentralized networks of knowledge circulation. I offer texts, materials, or critical interventions aimed not at providing answers but at provoking deeper questions.

 

If your project is guided by a clear, collective, ethical, and transformative commitment — and resonates with the paths described here — get in touch. I evaluate each proposal directly and in context. Collaborations may be one-off or sustained, in-person or remote, but are always grounded in horizontality, reciprocity, and mutual respect.
