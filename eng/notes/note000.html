A kind-of introduction

Note #000

Home > Tips & tools for researchers (and everybody else) > Note #000

 

Scientific, academic, and scholarly work is not solely about "doing science" or "producing knowledge". There is much more to it. By the time we realize and confront such a reality, we are usually in the middle of it, desperately looking around for tools to help us organize all that "much more". Which, honestly, can be a lot.

These tools (and tips, techniques, and hacks) reside within a little-explored area where academic scholarship intersects with information technologies (ITs), but also with communication and marketing. "Little-explored" may be an understatement: after all, Digital Humanities, the intersection of IT and Humanities, is an expanding field of study. However, there is a substantial lack of information about tools in many other disciplines — even within the humanities.

Here, librarians come to the rescue.

We, librarians, facilitate knowledge creation, recovery, organization, preservation, dissemination, and access. We also play a crucial role in curating, organizing, and providing access to information and resources that support research, learning, and discovery. We are not just "bookworms" anymore — or, at least, not all the time... A blend of technology-based know-how and of skills related to accessing information resources allows us to provide tips, tools, techniques, methodologies, and advice on how to produce, disseminate, and organize knowledge and memory.

That's what we do.

So... through this channel, every Monday, I'll be sharing with you short pieces on this topic that may be useful (or not...), covering (but not being limited to) the following topics:

  • How to identify ourselves in the academic world.
  • How to find information (including "taboos" like dark libraries).
  • How to organize that information and keep track of it.
  • How to use historical archives and "other" documents in our research — and add new voices and perspectives.
  • How to use different approaches to our research: from decolonialism to Epistemologías del Sur, and from margins to "rogue science".
  • How to be interdisciplinary, both in ideas and techniques — e.g., have you thought about giving GIS a try?
  • How to "talk to the machine" and interact with AI.
  • How to handle data ("da-what?") and not die trying...
  • How to handle copyright (don't, don't say "copy-what?")
  • How to show our work, our results, our context...
  • How to offer our work — going solo (independent consultant) if necessary, and creating a portfolio showcasing our processes and results.
  • How to find money.
  • How to find more money (it's never enough, isn't it?)
  • How to publish (without the fear of "perishing" as the only option).
  • How to go "open" — open access, open source, open science...
  • How to connect / network. With colleagues. Out there.
  • How to (out)reach the community and make our work useful.
  • How to stay updated. Yes, specially with technology,
  • And how to automatize everything (whenever possible. Do not expect magic here).

So... stay tuned!
