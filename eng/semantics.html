Semantics

Applied semantics is far more than a technical concern. It is a way of structuring what we know — and what we have forgotten — through systems capable of linking, preserving, and reusing knowledge across formats, disciplines, and time. This section presents my work as a semantic consultant specializing in ontology development, metadata modeling, and the design of data pipelines for institutions managing scientific collections, archival records, and biocultural memory.

My role begins where institutional knowledge systems break down: when metadata fails to represent what matters, when archival materials lack context, when scientific datasets are disconnected, or when classification models erase territories, relationships, and voices. In these spaces — often invisible or neglected — I develop solutions that combine technical precision, critical awareness, and ethical responsibility.

Here I document semantic enrichment processes applied to scientific collections, field archives, sound recordings, and oral histories. I present ontology models developed in Protégé, reconciliations performed with OpenRefine and RDFLib, RDF structures in JSON-LD, and concrete examples of how data workflows can transform the way institutions organize, access, and share knowledge. I work with standards such as Darwin Core, MODS, ABCD, EML, and Dublin Core, applying FAIR and CARE principles, and exploring tools like TK Labels to preserve context and data sovereignty.

Beyond results, this section includes reflections on data ethics, Southern epistemologies, multilingual representation, and decolonial methodologies. Each project featured here has been developed using real materials — especially from my work at the Smithsonian Tropical Research Institute — and aims both to solve technical needs and propose new frameworks for working with scientific knowledge and institutional memory.

This space is intended for professionals, institutions, and communities interested in advanced data management, meaningful preservation, and the design of semantic systems in service of biodiversity, culture, and history.
